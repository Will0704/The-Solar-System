Definition


Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and is also the smallest of the eight planets
in our solar system. For every 2 orbits the Sun, which takes around 88 Earth days,
Mercury completes three rotations of its axis. It is gravitationally locked and this
rotation is unique to the solar system.


Facts


- Mercury has been known to humanity since ancient times.
-A year in Mercury is 88 days, yet a Mercury day is 176 Earth days.
-Mercury orbits so quickly around the Sun that early civilizations believed it was actually two different stars.
-Mercury is the smallest planet in the solar system.
-After the Earth, Mercury is the seconds densest planet.
-Mercury is named afterthe messenger of the Roman gods.
-Astronomers didn't realize that Mercury was a planet until 1543 when Copernicus published his Sun-centered model of the solar system.
-The planet has just 38% of the gravity on Earth.
-Mercury has no moons or rings.
-It was once believed that a planet Vulcan existed betweenthe orbit of Mercury and the Sun.
-The orbit of Mercury is an ellipse rather than circular.
-Mercury is only the second hottest planet.
-Mercury does not experience any seasons.
-Mercury is the only planet which doesn't rotate exactly once every year.
-Mercury has a large iron corethat is around 40% of its volume.
-Only two spacecraft have ever visited Mercury.
-Mercury has a weak magnetic field.
-Mercury has more craters and impact marks that any other planet.
-Mercury has a very thin atmosphere.
